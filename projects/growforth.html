Slide Sanctuary

A choose-your-own adventure game about navigating a dreamlike world.

About:

Slide Sanctuary was created as part of a Digital Game Design class at WPI, and evolved over the course of multiple builds. It was created in Perlenspiel, an ultra-lightweight, Javascript-based game engine, which intentionally limits graphical possibilities to encourage intelligent, elegant design.

Players click on one of three rooms in the dungeon to move the room into the empty space on the board (like in a slide puzzle); players must rearrange the dungeon and gather keys in order to make a path to the staircase and escape the dungeon.

Development details:

Completed: 2020
Development time: ~6 weeks
Languages: Twine
Tools: Google Workspace

Team:

  • Lena Dias (myself) — lead programmer & designer

My role:

Lead Programmer

  • Programmed entirety of game, including player movement, lock-and-key system, inventory, audio, and level loading from image files
  • Managed builds and release versions

Lead Designer

  • Created core game idea
  • Developed 15+ levels, which steadily increase in difficulty and wordlessly teach the player the game mechanics
  • Drew title screen skull pixel art
  • Created survey materials for gathering feedback on intuitivity, usability, stability
  • Reassessed design according to playtesting feedback

My thoughts:

Honestly, I'm really happy with this one! Though graphically it looks unimpressive, the real challenge with this one was working around the intentionally-imposed limitations of the Perlenspiel engine; developers have very few pixels and a single line of text to work with, and thus need to be elegant when conveying mechanics to players. With the art depicting the controls on the title screen, sound feedback, and the traditional design language of an "inventory" bar, playtesters quickly got the hang of mechanics. I feel like I did a lot with very little with this project; there's solid mechanical progression, too!
